Aerodynamic Load Distribution — Downforce (CL) and Drag (CD) Contribution by Component.
Z-mode (high-downforce cornering configuration) vs. X-mode (ultra-low-drag straight-line configuration).
Total Z-mode: CL = 3.20, CD = 1.00 (L/D = 3.20). Total X-mode: CL = 2.80, CD = 0.65 (L/D = 4.31).
Component-level aerodynamic coefficients derived from Reynolds-Averaged Navier–Stokes (RANS) CFD simulations (ANSYS Fluent / OpenFOAM). Frontal reference area A = 1.5 m². Z-mode denotes the FIA-mandated high-downforce cornering configuration with closed wing elements; X-mode denotes the ultra-low-drag straight-line configuration with actuated (flattened) front and rear wing profiles.
Floor and venturi tunnels constitute the dominant downforce source (≈55% of total CL in Z-mode), consistent with 2026 ground-effect design philosophy. Active aero achieves 35% total drag reduction (CD: 1.00 → 0.65), primarily through rear wing flattening (−53%) and front wing adaptation (−57%). Diffuser contribution increases in X-mode due to reduced rear wing downwash interference, enhancing underbody flow expansion. Floor contribution remains invariant across modes — it is not subject to active actuation — validating the ground-effect-dominant design philosophy.
